# Designing a Multi-Layered Ransomware Defence Architecture for FinTech Infrastructure

**Author:** [Jesutofunmi Akanmu]  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Type:** Research Article  

---

## Abstract

The financial technology sector has emerged as a primary target for ransomware threat actors owing to its concentration of high-value data, dependence on real-time payment infrastructure, and reliance on complex third-party integration ecosystems. This article analyses the ransomware threat landscape as it applies to FinTech organisations, mapping the adversary kill chain to the MITRE ATT&CK Enterprise framework to identify the principal techniques employed across the attack lifecycle. It proposes a structured, five-layer defence-in-depth architecture encompassing identity and access security, network segmentation, endpoint detection and response, backup and recovery strategy, and incident response preparedness. The architecture is examined through the lens of the defence-in-depth principle, demonstrating how the interdependency of layers compensates for the inherent limitations of any single control. The article further considers the practical challenges of deploying this architecture within FinTech environments, including budget constraints, legacy system integration, multi-jurisdictional regulatory obligations, and operational change management.

**Keywords:** ransomware, FinTech security, defence-in-depth, MITRE ATT&CK, zero trust architecture, privileged access management, incident response, financial sector cybersecurity
